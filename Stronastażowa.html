Virtual internships

Poland

Poland | EN

Our virtual internships are short, interactive job simulations that show you what working on real matters can look like. You’ll complete tasks inspired by everyday work in a global law firm — at your own pace — and get a practical feel for different practice areas before you apply.

Below you’ll find two simulations available in this mock: Energy and Employment (Labour law).

Who can join?

Anyone curious about commercial law, business, or how legal teams support complex projects. The simulations are designed to be accessible and useful both for students and early-career candidates.

Access
Free & online
Open from anywhere. Complete tasks on your laptop in a browser, whenever it suits you.
Time
Self-paced
No deadlines in this mock — work through the steps in your own rhythm.
Outcome
Practical insight
Understand the work, the language, and the typical documents you’ll see in real cases.

Step into the world of law

In each simulation you’ll act like a junior team member supporting a matter. The tasks are simplified for learning, but based on realistic steps and decisions — from spotting risks to drafting and reviewing documents.

Simulation 1 • Energy

Energy: Projects & regulation

Step into an energy project scenario where legal advice supports investment decisions, compliance, and risk management. You’ll work through a practical set of tasks that mirror how lawyers structure and protect a deal.

  1. Identify regulatory touchpoints and the key approvals needed for a project.
  2. Review a simplified term sheet and flag allocation of risk and responsibility.
  3. Spot common contractual issues (scope, timelines, change control, liability).
  4. Draft short notes for the team: risks, questions for the client, next steps.

Simulation 2 • Employment

Labour law: People & workplace (DEMO - BRAK KONTENTU)

Advise on a workplace scenario where legal teams balance business needs with employee rights and compliance obligations. You’ll practise analysing facts, applying rules, and preparing clear, structured advice.

  1. Assess a set of facts and determine which employment risks are most urgent.
  2. Review draft communication to employees and improve clarity and compliance.
  3. Identify documentation gaps and propose a checklist of required steps.
  4. Prepare a short summary for a partner: risks, options, recommended approach.

What you’ll gain

Complete the simulations in your own time and use them to feel more confident with legal tasks, documents, and how teams work. This is a mock page, but the learning goals mirror real programmes.

  • Apply your studies to real-world style scenarios and commercial context.
  • Build confidence by tackling tasks you’d see in a junior role.
  • Improve your ability to explain risks clearly and propose next steps.
  • Get a better sense of which practice area feels right for you.
  • Strengthen your CV story: “I’ve tried the work and I know what it involves.”

Skills you’ll learn

Each simulation is built around a small roadmap of skills that are useful in applications and in day-to-day work.

Research

Develop your ability to conduct legal research and extract what matters for the client’s decision.

Drafting

Write concise notes, risk summaries, and improvements to documents or communications.

Due diligence

Learn how teams check documents and spot legal / commercial issues early.

Reviewing

Identify inconsistencies, gaps, and unclear language — and propose fixes.

Gain confidence summarising issues for senior team members and preparing clear, actionable next steps.

Commercial awareness

Understand how legal advice connects to business risk, timelines, and stakeholder goals.
